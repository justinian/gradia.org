Long ago in the dawn of Corinth's existance, a man came out of the northlands; he spoke like the northmen and carried their oriental culture with them, but he was not oriental-born. The man, beset upon by the black naga warriors of the north - the <i>ninja</i>, was protected by Corinthian guards. Grateful, the man traveled to the capitol and presented himself to the king. Introducing himself as <i>Shintatsu</i>, the man gave his loyalty to the king's service, and became known as a skillful warrior. Eventually, Shintatsu took on apprentices and founded a group of Samurai warriors sworn to defend Corin and her king. The Order of the Dragon is the continuation of that group of Samurai, still in service protecting the Crown, as well as the Parliment. While not quite a national secret, the Order of the Dragon keeps a low profile and small numbers - unlike their elite counterparts to the north. Training to join the Order begins when the student is still a young child, and is a long, hard road. The Order of Lucient to the north may be the most feared of the area, but the Samurai of the Order of the Dragon are more than a match for a Red Guard or two.
