Galen's Crossing, first settled by the Empire of Narin in 240IA, long marked
the far southern boundary of the Empire. Around 305IA, it was the center of an
expansionist program for the empire.

* **TN Village** (Population 200)
    No Government, Rumormongering Citizens, Insular
* Corruption -1
* Crime -2
* Economy -1
* Law +0
* Lore +2
* Society -2
* Danger 0

##  Wards 

The town is made up of four wards south of the Dhetëlin river, and the Narin
military outpost to the north.

###  Military Fort 

The military ward is protected by strong walls and a large gate, though it is
mostly open along the river banks. The South Imperial Road comes in from the
north through the gate, and continues through the fort over a large stone
bridge across the river.

Notable buildings here:

* Army administration building (Good)
* Two soldiers' stables and corral (Common)
* Officers' stables (Good)
* Soldiers' barracks (Common)
* Officers' barracks (Good)
* Workshop: Carpetners (Good)
* Workshop: Blacksmith (Good)

###  River Ward 

Most of the southern river bank comprises the river ward.

Notable buildings here:

* 2 Inns (Common)
* Temple of Mara (Common)
* "The Lonesome Fisher" Tavern (Common)
* Workshop: Carpenters (Poor)

###  Market Ward 

To the south of the River Ward, the largest ward of the town houses both the
small bazaar, and most of the shops in town.

Notable buildings:

* Inn (Good)
* Church of the Sun King (Fancy)
* Good Shops: Fishmonger, Furrier, Mercer/Tailor
* Common Shops: Butcher, Diary Seller, Grocer, Wine Merchant
* Stable (Good)
* Tavern (Good)

###  Eastern Ward 

Along the river bank to the east of the river ward is the eastern ward, which
predominantly features the town cemetery, overlooked by a hill and the temple.

Notable buildings:

* Temple to a Sun-saint (Good)
* Stable (Common)
* "The Whiskey Maiden" Tavern (Common)

###  Southern Ward 

To the south of the Market Ward, the less pleasant industry of the town is
relegated to the southern ward. This area makes up the outskirts of town before
the settlement gives way entirely to farms.

Notable buildings:
* Temple (Common)
* Shop: Butcher (poor)
* Tavern (common)
* Common Workshops: Blacksmith, Brewer, Tanner, Wheelwrights
