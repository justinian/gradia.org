The Kelsheen are a race of horse nomads that wander the endless green of the Northern Plains.  They were once known as the ''Hilshee'', a peaceful people who farmed the lands now conquered by the Xar... now driven from their homeland and roaming the plains.  They are a stout, pious people who must avoid the territorial Wemics, guard against the brutal Gnolls, and beware the wild magics of the Weirdlands.  Their ways are ancient and their spirits are unbreakable.

The Kelsheen are divided into many tribes, either by proximity, bloodlines or similar ideologies.  A typical tribe consists of a chief, a mystic, numerous priests and fifty to two-hundred families.  The tribes themselves range from peaceful, to isolated, to bloodthirsty.  It is rare for one tribe to attack another for an extended period of time.

Horses are vital to Kelsheen life.  To a tribe, they act as beasts of burden, mounts, and members of the family.  The Kelsheen are expert breeders, and it is rumored that Kelsheen mounts are some of the smartest and strongest in the world.  Legend has it that one tribe's horses contains the blessed blood of celestials and unicorns.

They live in tumbledown tents that can be easily packed onto their horses when it is time to roam once more.  Traditionally, men form hunting and patrol parties, while women remain home to protect the children.  Battle priests and other dedicated defenders also remain with their tribes, guarding against dangers and teaching youngsters the ways of their people.

The Kelsheen worship a pair of centaur-like gods they call the ''Allasheen''... Oddamura the Warrior, father and protector, and Almira the Wanderer, mother and shepherd... who gallop across the sky as the day and night, guiding their children through hardship and suffering.

Once a year, during the full moon, the Kelsheen tribes gather for the annual ''Kultak'', or "Reunion."  The location for the ''Kultak'' is different each year, and must be determined by the mystics and priests.  The Kelsheen reunite to share food, drink and stories with their kin.  It is also a meeting ground for the Kelsheen chiefs, who discuss tribal relations and what they have seen among the Northern Plains.

#  Tribes of Note 
**The Moheerla:** The largest of the Kelsheen tribes.  The Moheerla are famous for their piety, hospitality, and bravery in battle.
* *The Razghala:** Some of the finest archers in the world are of the Razghala.  They have mastered the art of mounted archery, and rarely share their techniques with outsiders.
* *The Doma:** The Doma have never forgotten the Xar invasion.  This violent and aggressive tribe regularly assaults Xar settlements, rarely leaving survivors.
* *The Bikta:** The ''Umar Kelshi'' is the weapon presented to the Kelsheen by the ''Allasheen''.  It is their most sacred relic, and the Bikta are its sworn guardians.
* *The Shintula:** The Shintula have wandered too near the Weirdlands.  They are a people affected, and afflicted, by the wild magics of those blasted lands.
* *The Alheera:** The smallest of the Kelsheen tribes.  They are known for their kindness, generosity, and advanced knowledge in the healing arts.

#  Cultural Notes 
* "Family" to a Kelsheen includes their spouses / parents, siblings, horses, close friends and the rest of their tribe (in typically descending order of importance, although a Kelsheen may hold his horse closer to his heart than his brat sister).
 * Horses are considered sacred animals and members of the family. They are never eaten. It is considered an act of heresy and cannibalism. When a horse dies its remains are cremated and scattered to the winds.
 * The Kelsheen grow their hair like a horse's mane. They do not cut it or braid it in any way, for they view it as a sign of restraint, similar to a collar around a dog's neck. Ponytails are acceptable for their practicality while riding, and for the priests.  To offer to braid or cut a Kelsheen's hair is considered very offensive.
 * Kelsheen typically wear loose, austere clothing. It shields their skin from the harsh sun and biting winds of the Northern Plains. Veils and simple headdresses are common in worse weather. A chief wears a sash adorned with small gifts from his/her tribe's families. ''Allasheen'' priests always wear one lock of hair in a ponytail, secured by a small ring of beads (dyed white for Oddamura, black for Almira). Mystics wear whatever they please.
 * The tumbledown tents the Kelsheen live in when not traveling are called ''taktaks''. The smallest hold one person (typically the tribe's mystic), while the largest have been known to hold up to twenty people (usually assembled for the annual ''Kultak'').
  * The Kelsheen never, ever strike an opponent's mount in combat. Nothing is considered more dishonorable and cowardly.
 * Arcane magic is frowned upon and mistrusted.  Wizards and Sorcerers, especially the rare ones within the tribes, are viewed with a cautious eye at best. The magic of Bards is tolerated. The <i>Shintula Nakta</i> are pitied, but never outcast or exiled unless their "gifts" make them too violent.
 * The Kelsheen do not use money of any kind. They trade with one another, and their system of exchange changes as often as their location. Kelsheen share everything with one another, often exchanging personal gifts and trinkets.
