* *Appearance**

Molitor appears as a stately older gentleman, a silver-haired
human of healthy proportions who usually is dressed in nice
clothing - he carries himself as bourgeoisie instead of
aristocracy.
* *Notes**

Molitor was a powerful wizard in the days when the gods
interacted more in the lives of men. He was a follower of the
gods, and a man who strived for good. However, when The First
War broke out and mankind had to defend itself against a
seemingly infinite onslaught of goblinoids, Molitor realized
his position. He created a series of enchantments, similar to
today's process of becoming a Lich, only much more powerful.
Molitor was the hero of The First War, but found himself
slipping. The power he had gained was eating at his soul, and
making him much more the corrupt soul that it reflected.
Molitor also realized that he was unable to bring himself to
death, so he devised a plan to keep himself from ever
becoming a menace to mankind instead of its hero.

In his time, Molitor had discovered that he could funnel off
a portion of his power to another, creating a less-powerful
creature like himself. He also found that because the power
he gave them was his own, a mental and magical bond began to
form between him and his "children." Molitor created four
children, and after a time of being with them as a messianic
figure and a teacher, bequeathed upon them the task of
guarding his body. He had created a secret tomb, into which
he locked himself for all eternity, guarded by his children.
