Geoffet Sosae was an adventurer, 50 years ago, during the
Narin War, and he and his party played a crucial role in the rise of the
house of Dunkirk, and led troops in several battles to drive Narin forces
from Corin soil (no, this is not a character of any of ours, he's new).
He, and one of his adventuring companions were half elven. They grew close
to eachother during their travels, and married after the final victory of
Corin, and had their marriage blessed by the king himself.

The king granted Geoffet lordship in return for his services, and he
retired to live out a life of ease and happiness in the port town of Brax
Landing. He had four children with Ibis Mistrata, his wife. The children
were all half-elven, and blessed with a long life from their blood line.
Ibis was not contented to a life of 'homemaking', and her wanderlust (that
first caused her to adventure) took hold of her later in life. She set off
with her youngest daughter (Feawen?) to journey to (somewhere) to visit
(someone important to her, family? friends?) and introduce her child.
Feawen was young, but old enough to travel.

The caravan they travelled with was assalted by Eergu and Ibis fought hard to defend her child, but the attacking
forces were too much for her and a bunch of caravan guards, and soon, they
all fell but Ibis, who was mortally wounded. Even so hurt, she took her
child and fled the scene of the massacre, hoping to find help before she
finally died. The fate of the young daughter is unknown (to me, cuz this
is where you take over her story).

When Geoffet heard of the attack, it was too late for him to send help,
but he gathered a massive search party, and scoured the countryside for
his wife and daughter. After nearly a year of searching for the pair, and
for those responsible (who were executed when they could be ferreted out),
Geoffet retired to his estate in despair to finally mourn for his wife.

One of Geoffet's old companions was a priest of the Windlord, a man by the
name of Justas Dunharrow. He married a Greaden woman by the name of
Yallia Festen about 5 years after the end of the Narin War, and they
settled in to roost on a farm in Greade, near to the Weirdlands. Yallia
was a wizard, trained by the warwizards of Greade, and sought to study the
Weirdlands in her retirement from adventuring. Her daughter was Merila
Dunharrow, who grew to be a beautiful young woman.

When Merila was a grown woman, Justas felt his feet grow restless, and
desired to travel once more. He wished to visit his old friends who had
settled in Brax Landing, so he prepared for the long journey. Marila wish
ed to go to, having heard of all the adventures they had shared, and she
wanted to meet these heroes of Justas' stories. Yallia stayed to watch
the house and continue her studies as her husband and daughter journeyd to
Corin.

Their journey was uneventful, but when they arrived, Justas got the first
word that Ibis had died. Telling the news was like reopenning a wound for
Geoffet, and grief overwhelmed him, but he resolved to be a good host for
his old friend and his beautiful daughter, who was now of a relative age
with Geoffet.

Justus and Merila wintered in Brax Landing, and during that time, Merila
and Geoffet grew close. When it was time to return to Greade, Geoffet felt
a new grief, the grief of parting with Merila, who had been so kind to
him. He resolved then to ask for her hand in marriage. Justas granted his
blessing to the marriage, and Merila was in love as well. In the spring,
they sent word to Yallia, who travelled down to Brax Landing, and Geoffet
and Marila were married in the summer. Geoffet's children mostly liked
Marila and supported the marriage, all but Arjuna, the eldest. There was
the chance that Marila would bear a son to Geoffet, and that could cause
issues of favoritism to destroy the family, and cause a succession
dispute.

Justas and Yallia returned to Greade that Fall, though they kept in
touch. Geoffet and Marila had a happy marriage, and as Arjuna feared, it
was not long before there were children once again running about the
house. The first was Bentell, a bookish child who inherited his
grandmother's love of mysticism and learning. He was sent to school in
Thelarion, a great honor made possible by his grandmother's connections
there. The second child was a daughter, named Ibis. Lastly, Argali was
born, and now the family had grown large indeed.

Two years after Argali's birth, Hercal, the second son of Ibis, left home
to fight with the Corin forces in the east against the rising orc forces
there. Five years he was away from home when Geoffet got news that he had
died in battle. Geoffet's heart nearly broke, and placed even more of his
hopes upon Arjuna and Argali, the sons that still lived with him. Arjuna
has wanted to go to the battles as well, but was held home by his
responsibilities as the eldest son. He regreted not fighting beside his
brother, and felt immense guilt.

Argali grew up idolizing Arjuna, the epitomy of young nobility. Trained in
the sword, and the art of ruling with justice and mercy, Arjuna seemed
destined for greatness, and Argali, in his youth, often pestered his
brother to teach him the blade. Arjuna was annoyed most of the time by his
younger half-brother's badgering, but occasionally he agreed to teach him,
in a strange way. He said that the best thing to know in a fight is how
not to get hit, and would not allow Argali to weild a sparring stick in
their 'lessons'. Arjuna made a game of trying to hit the boy, lightly, but
enough to sting, while Argali made a game of dodging him, and predicting
his moves.

One day, Arjuna agreed that Argali was old enough to try using a sparing
blade, and had promised to teach him the weapon when he was done his
chores. It was not to be, for that day, Justas arrived in Brax Landing,
bearing news that Yallia had passed on. Geoffet welcomed into the
household, and Justas immediately noticed Argali, as if seeing a beacon
after a long night. "That is Argali, my grandson?" he asked. "Certainly.
He is a smart lad, quiet but quick. He'll be a good man when he grows up."
"Indeed," Justas replied. "He has a future with the Windlord as surely as
I did. Perhaps he will even achieve more than I. For all my glory and
travels, I chose the mortal life, but this one..."

With that began Argali's training as a Skywatcher. No more could he spar
with Arjuna, or widdle away his days. He spent all his hours in study,
prayer, and meditation. He learned to dream himself into the dreamscapes
of the Gods, and even to find the High Aerie where the Skyfather waits. He
delighted most in the dreams of flying, soring over the land that passed
underneath him. He would sometimes sleep late, revelling so much in these
dreams, where he could feel the presence of the Sky Lord.

And now, he is still little more than a boy, but his training is complete.
He is ready for the Long Road, the period of a Skywatcher's life where he
must journey to the far corners of the world, and 'speak to the Four
Winds', which is a cryptic quest some take too literally, and some take
too metaphorically.

For those who are counting, there are 5 children (not including the
possibility of Feawen) remaining in this family. Arjuna, his sister
Aliandra, Bentell, who is in Thelarion, Ibis and Argali.
