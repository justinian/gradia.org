#  Damage and Wounds 
For Gradia, we have simplified the rules governing damage and wounds.

When a character is hit, they perform a Toughness Save to see if the damage is serious or negligible.
* *Toughness Save** = d20 + Constitution + Feats + Armor - Toughness save penalties **''versus**'' DC15 + Attack's Damage Bonus
* *Toughness Saving Throw**
||**Result**||||**Non-lethal**||||**Lethal**||
||succeeds||||no effect||||no effect||
||fails by 1-4||||light stun||||light wound||
||fails by 5-9||||serious stun||||serious wound||
||fails by 10-14|||| concussed||||disabled||
||fails by 15-19||||unconscious||||dying||
||fails by 20+||||unconscious||||dead||

Stuns and wounds collect on a player, and their effects stack. For each light or serious stun a character has, they suffer a -1 penalty to Toughness save rolls to resist non-lethal damage. For each light or serious wound a character has, they suffer a -1 penalty to all Toughness save rolls to resist any damage, lethal or non-lethal.

A character who has at least one serious wound or stun is Staggered. A character who is Staggered may make one move-equivalent or standard action per turn without penalty. The character may act normally (taking a full round action or the usual standard and move-equivalent actions), but suffers an additional serious wound (if wounded) or serious stun (if stunned) at the end of the turn.

A character who is Concussed is Staggered, but will become Unconscious at the end of the turn if they take ANY action.

A character who is Disabled is Staggered, but will begin Dying at the end of the turn if they take ANY action.

An Unconscious character may take no action and is unaware of their surroundings until a successful recovery check.

A Dying character is unconscious, may take no action, and is unaware of their surroundings. At the beginning of every round a character is in the Dying condition, they make a Constitution check (DC10). On a failed check, the character dies. On a successful check, they are alive at least until the next round where they must make another check. If they succeed by 10 or more, they stabilize, and their condition becomes Unconscious and Disabled.

If a Concussed character fails a Toughness save against non-lethal damage by 10-14, they become unconscious. If a Disabled character fails a Toughness save against lethal damage by 10-14, their condition changes to Dying. If a dying character fails a Toughness save by 10+, they die.

When a character is Unconscious, any further non-lethal damage is considered lethal damage.
