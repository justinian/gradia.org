- "Family" to a Kelsheen includes their spouses / parents, siblings, horses, close friends and the rest of their tribe (in typically descending order of importance, although a Kelsheen may hold his horse closer to his heart than his brat sister).



- Horses are considered sacred animals and members of the family. They are never eaten. Ever. It is considered an act of heresy and cannibalism. When a horse dies its remains are cremated and scattered to the winds.



- The Kelsheen grow their hair like a horse's mane. They do not cut it or braid it in any way, for they view it as a sign of restraint, similar to a collar around a dog's neck. Ponytails are acceptable for their practicality while riding, and for the priests.  To offer to braid or cut a Kelsheen's hair is considered very offensive.



- Kelsheen typically wear loose, austere clothing. It shields their skin from the harsh sun and biting winds of the Northern Plains. Veils and simple headdresses are common in worse weather. A chief wears a sash adorned with small gifts from his/her tribe's families. <i>Allasheen</i> priests always wear one lock of hair in a ponytail, secured by a small ring of beads (dyed white for Oddamura, black for Almira). Mystics wear whatever they please.



- The tumbledown tents the Kelsheen live in when not traveling are called "<i>taktaks</i>." The smallest hold one person (typically the tribe's mystic), while the largest have been known to hold up to twenty people (usually assembled for the annual <i>Kultak</i>).


 
- The Kelsheen never, ever strike an opponent's mount in combat. Nothing is considered more dishonorable and cowardly.


 
- The worst insult in the Kelsheen language is "<i>mukta alla fukta shellateen</i>," which loosely translates to "May your horse trample you and shit on your corpse." Rival Kelsheen have killed one another over this.


 
- Because men and women are equals in every way, it is not considered dishonorable for a man to strike a woman if she has insulted him. Women do the same to men when they are insulted.



- The annual <i>Kultak</i> is the most important event in the Kelsheen culture. To miss the <i>Kultak</i> is a great sorrow for many Kelsheen. They have been known to sink into a deep depression when the time of the <i>Kultak</i> passes them.



- Arcane magic is frowned upon and mistrusted.  Wizards and Sorcerers, especially the rare ones within the tribes, are viewed with a cautious eye at best. The magic of Bards is tolerated. The <i>Shintula Nakta</i> are pitied, but never outcast or exiled unless their "gifts" make them too violent.


 
- The Kelsheen do not use money of any kind. They trade with one another, and their system of exchange changes as often as their location. Kelsheen share everything with one another, often exchanging personal gifts and trinkets.
