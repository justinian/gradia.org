<b>Master at Arms</b>





The Master at Arms is an artisan of combat, skilled at turning any thing he holds into a deadly weapon. The Master at Arms can be a myrmidon captain to a powerful lord, or a wandering brawler, but what makes him unique is his knowledge and understanding of battle.





<b>Prerequisites</b>:




<b>Base Attack Bonus</b>: +6




<b>Feats</b>: Exotic Weapon Proficiency (Any two), Expertise, must be proficient in all Simple and Martial Weapons.





<b>Hit Dice</b>: d10




<b>Class Skills</b>: Balance, Climb, Craft, Handle Animal, Intimidate, Jump, Knowledge (Warfare and Tactics), Ride, Sense Motive, Spot, Swim, Tumble




<b>Skills per Level</b>: 2+INT Modifier

<b>Class Features</b>





<b>Weapons and Armor</b>: The Master at Arms gains no weapon or armor proficiencies other than those granted by Weapon Skill.





<b>Weapon Skill</b>: The Master at Arms is less concerned with training with a single weapon than knowing how to use any object the best way possible. The Master at Arms suffers no proficiency penalty for using an unfamiliar weapon. Further, he suffers no penalty for using an improvised weapons.





<b>Fast Hands</b>: At 2nd level, the Master at Arms may, when wielding any weapon (including improvised weapons) and using the Full Attack action, make an additional attack at his highest attack bonus with that weapon. However, all his attacks this round suffer a -2 penalty. This is an extraordinary ability.





<b>Improved Weapon Skill</b>: At 3rd Level, the Master at Arms gains the Extraordinary Ability to use a melee weapon's (including improvised weapons) innate advantages to enhanced effect. He may use one of the following effects in a single round. Changing effects is a free action that does not provoke an attack of opportunity:




- Add a +1 competence bonus to Attack. At 5th level, this bonus increases to +2.




- Add a +1 competence bonus to Damage. At 5th level, this bonus increases to +2.




- If the weapon is one size larger than the Master at Arms, he may make it a reach weapon (with reach equal to other reach weapons of that size). For example, a Greatsword would have a 10' reach. If the weapon already has reach, this affect does not stack.




- Add a +2 competence bonus to Disarm attempts. This stacks with the weapon's preexisting bonus to disarm, if any. At 5th level, this bonus increases to +4.




- The weapon can be used to attempt a Trip Attack with a +2 competence bonus. If the Trip Attack fails, the Master at Arms may drop the weapon to avoid being tripped himself. At 5th level, the competence bonus increases to +4.





<b>Appraisal</b>: At 4th level, the Master at Arms can identify weapons and opponents with ease. The Master at Arms gains a +10 competence bonus to Sense Motive rolls to determine an opposing character's level or total attack and damage bonus with primary weapon. Also, the Master at Arms can determine the quality of a weapon by wielding it. The Master at Arms must hold the weapon in his hands, and make a practice swing, thrust or jab as appropriate to the weapon. He can identify the weapon's innate enhancement bonus (if any) or if the weapon is masterwork. This is an extraordinary ability.





<b>Supreme Weapon Skill</b>: At 5th Level, the Master at Arms gains the ability to use a melee weapon's (including improvised weapons) innate advantages to their greatest effect. He may use one of the following effects in a single round (Note: This is in addition to any effects granted the weapon by Improved Weapon Skill). Changing effects is a free action that does not promote an attack of opportunity:




- The weapon is considered magical for purposes of damage reduction, with an effective bonus of +1. If the weapon is already magical, this has no effecct.




- Increase the weapon's critical range by 1 (note, this is not a doubling).




- Increase the weapon's critical multiplier by 1 (note, this is not a doubling either).




- Wield a weapon up to one size larger than the Master at Arms one-handed.




- Deal subdual damage instead of normal damage, or normal damage instead of subdual damage, at no penalty to damage or attack roll.




- Gain a dodge bonus to armor class based on weapon size. If the weapon is one or more sizes smaller that the Master at Arms, +1. If it is the same size category, +2. If it is one size larger, +3. If an opponent misses the character as a result of this bonus ONLY, the weapon is struck and takes damage as if it were the target of the initial attack.
