In the ever twilight ice of Othalia, in the darkest night of the Winter, the restless dead return, consumed by hatred of the living. They are the Craven, those who feared and despaired in the face of battle, those who fell, struck in the back as they ran. But now they know no fear.





Fetahar, the Othalian god of death and fear, is believed to have created the Craven as an army that grows, rather than diminishes, in battle. It is believed he will use this army to battle the ghostly warriors of Vodn, the Slain God, at the end of the world.





Craven is a template that can be applied to any humanoid who, in life, worshipped the Othalian gods, and who died fleeing from battle. The creature's type changes to Undead. It retains most type modifiers (such as Fire or Aquatic) but loses alignment type modifiers (such as Good) and
type modifiers that indicate kind (such as Goblinoid or Reptilian). It uses all the base creature's statistics and special abilities except as noted here.





<b>
HD
</b>
: Hit die type changes to d12. If the creature had fewer than 3 hit dice, increase hit dice to 3d12.





<b>
Speed
</b>
: Use creature's base speed, and add 10ft.





<b>
AC
</b>
: Creature gains a +3 natural armor bonus to AC. This stacks with any existing natural armor the creature might have had.





<b>
Attacks
</b>
: A Craven retains all the natural attacks, manufactured weapons, and weapon proficiencies of the base creature, and also gains a slam attack.





<b>
Damage
</b>
: Natural and manufactured weapons deal damage normally. A slam attack deals damage depending on the Craven's size. (Small creatures do 1d4, Medium sized creatures do 1d6, Large creatures do 1d8, Huge creautres do 2d6, and so on.) For damage purposes, a slam counts as a 2-handed attack. If the creature already had a slam attack, use the higher damage.





<b>
Special Attacks
</b>
: The Craven retains all the base creature's special attacks, and gains:
<ul>
<li>
<i>Wail of Terror</i> (Su): Once per day, when the Craven emits a moaning wail, all
creatures within a 30' radius must make a Will Save (DC 10 + 1/2 the
Craven's HD + the Craven's Cha modifier) or be Shaken (-2 morale penalty
to attacks, skill checks and saves) for 1d4 + the Craven's Cha modifier
rounds. This is a supernatural, mind-effecting ability. (note: since fear
effects are cumulative, multiple Craven may use this ability at once, and
if the targets fail their saves twice, they become Frightened, and
attempt to flee. If they become Shaken again, they become Panicked, and
have a 50% chance of dropping anything they are carrying and so on. This
means a pack of Craven can often very easily kill fleeing opponents,
making more Craven.)
</li>

</ul>

<b>
Special Qualities
</b>
: The Craven retains all special qualities of the base
creature except those subtypes it loses (such as Good) and gains the
Undead type. In addition, it gains these special qualities:
<ul>
<li>
Fast Healing: The wounds of a Craven close quickly. The Craven heals 2
points of damage every round, though it cannot grow back lost limbs.
</li>
<li>Unnerving Presence: Animals and characters with the Alertness feat 'get
the chills' when a Craven is within 100'. They do not have to see or be
aware of the Craven to feel them. They will not neccessasirly know the
cause of the sensation, though one can learn what it means.
</li>
<li>Create Spawn: Any creature killed by a Craven while Frightened of Panicked
returns from death as a Craven within 1d3 days unless the body is
detroyed by fire or dismemberment.
</li>
<li>
Daylight Vulnerability: A Craven exposed to daylight, either natural or
from the Daylight spell, behaves as if Panicked, and remains so until it
is out of the light.
</li>
</ul>

<b>
Saves
</b>
: The Craven uses the base creature's saves, though it no longer
recieves a Con bonus to Fort saves (big deal, it never makes Fort saves
anymore anyway).





<b>
Abilities
</b>
: Both Strength and Dexterity increase by 2, and it has no
Constitution score.





<b>
Skills
</b>
: As base creature, and the Craven receives a +4 competence bonus to
Sense Motive rolls to sense fear.





<b>
Feats
</b>
: As base creature.





<b>
CR
</b>
: As base creature, +1.
