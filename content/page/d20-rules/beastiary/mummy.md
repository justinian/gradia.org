(Based on the revised Mummy presented by Wizards of the Coast, here is a Template for mummies that can be applied to any humanoid or animal)

</p>





A mummy is a preserved corpse, animated to serve in death as it did in life by the dark gods who they worshiped. Often set to guard the tombs of the God Kings of Rygalta, they are powerful combatants, and will relentlessly attack anyone who interferes with the performance of their duties.

</p>





In Rygalta, mummies are commonly wrapped in funeral shrouds colored to signify their duties in life. Slaves and house servants wear white, warriors wear red, and magicians and priests wear black. Over time, the dyes fade and cloth yellows, so the position the oldest mummies held in life are not easily identified. Often, they will be set to perform the same task in death. Another part of the enbalming process is a large amount of herbs and chemicals, so they often radiate a pungent odor, not unlike a spice cabinet.

</p>





Mummy is a template that can be applied to any humanoid or animal who's body was subjected to an enbalming ritual, binding the soul and preserving the body. The creature's type changes to Undead. It retains most type modifiers (such as Fire or Aquatic) but loses alignment type modifiers (such as Good) and type modifiers that indicate kind (such as Goblinoid or Reptilian). It uses all the base creature's statistics and special abilities except as noted here.

</p>





<b>
HD
</b>
: Hit die type changes to d12. If the creature had fewer than 8 hit dice, increase hit dice to 8d12.

</p>





<b>
Speed
</b>
: As base creature, subtracting 10 feet.

</p>





<b>
AC
</b>
: Creature gains a +10 natural armor bonus to AC. This stacks with any existing natural armor the creature might have had.

</p>





<b>
Attacks
</b>
: A Mummy retains all the natural attacks, manufactured weapons, and weapon proficiencies of the base creature, and also gains a slam attack. If the creature gained hit dice from this template, increase the base attack bonus to +4, or use the base creature's, which ever is higher. A slam attack deals damage depending on the Craven's size. (Small creatures do 1d4, Medium sized creatures do 1d6, Large creatures do 1d8, Huge creautres do 2d6, and so on.) For damage purposes, a slam counts as a 2-handed attack. If the creature already had a slam attack, use the higher damage. In addition, any successful attack curses the target with <i>Mummy Rot</i> (see below).

</p>





<b>
Special Attacks
</b>
: The Mummy retains all the base creature's special attacks, and gains:

</p>

<ul>

<li>
Mummy Rot (Su): Supernatural disease -- slam, Fortitude DC 14 + CHA modifier, incubation period 1 minute; damage 1d6 Con and 1d6 Cha. Unlike normal diseases, mummy rot continues until the victim reaches Constitution 0 (and dies) or is cured as described below. Mummy rot is a powerful curse, not a natural disease. A character attempting to cast any conjuration (healing) spell on a creature afflicted with mummy rot must succeed on a DC 20 caster level check, or the spell has no effect on the afflicted character. To eliminate mummy rot, the curse must first be broken with break enchantment or remove curse (requiring a DC 20 caster level check for either spell), after which a caster level check is no longer necessary to cast healing spells on the victim, and the mummy rot can be magically cured as any normal disease. An afflicted creature who dies of mummy rot shrivels away into sand and dust that blow away into nothing at the first wind
</li>

<li>
Despair (Su): At the mere sight of a mummy, the viewer must succeed on  a Will save (DC 14 + CHA modifier) or be paralyzed with fear for 1d4 rounds. Whether or not the save is successful, that creature cannot be affected again by the same mummy's despair ability for 24 hours.
</li>

</ul>





<b>
Special Qualities
</b>
: The Mummy retains all special qualities of the base
creature except those subtypes it loses (such as Good) and gains the Undead type and traits. In addition, it gains these special qualities:
<ul>
<li>
Damage Reduction : 5/--
</li>
<li>Darkvision (60 ft)
</li>
<li>Vulnerability to Fire
</li>
</ul>

<b>
Saves
</b>
: The Mummy uses the base creature's saves, though it no longer recieves a CON modifier to Fort saves. If the creature gained Hit Dice from this Template, it uses Fort +2, Ref +2, Will +6 as its base saves, or it's original saves, whichever individual save is higher.

</p>





<b>
Abilities
</b>
: Strength is increased by 14, Intelligence is reduced by 4 (this will reduce the base creature's skill points, as it looses it's memories of mortality), Wisdom and Charisma are both increased by 4, and it has no Constitution score.

</p>





<b>
Skills
</b>
: As base creature, excepting those skills lost due to the Intelligence decrease. 

</p>





<b>
Feats
</b>
: As base creature. If the creautre gained Hit Dice from this class, they recieve extra feats until they have three total feats (excluding bonus feats granted by class levels).

</p>





<b>
CR
</b>
: As base creature, +4.
