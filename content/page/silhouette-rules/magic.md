#  Types of Magic 
Reality in Gradia is based on belief. Magic is merely the effect of particularly strong beliefs upon the world. Magic comes in many styles, based on the belief system and culture of the magic-weilder.

##  Spell-based Magic 
Spell-based magic consists of taking an action to produce an external effect on the world. The caster's belief system determines what spell effects he can learn, and which attribute he uses for his Casting skill.

Common Casting systems are Wizardy (Casting linked to KNO), which is learned arcane knowledge and casting through formulaic combinations of gesture, incantation and ritual; and Divinity (Casting linked to PSY), which draws its power from devout prayer and iconography, such as holy symbols.
 * **Magical Aptitude** ''(5 point perk)'' Allows the character to take the Casting skill.
* Casting a spell takes 3 actions.
 * The actions but must be consecutive.
 * The actions can be rushed as with normal Silhouette multiple-action rules.
 * The mutliple-action penalty for rushing a spell is only applied once.
 * Taking 6 non-rushed actions (1 minute) to cast the spell gives a +1 bonus.
 * Taking 60 non-rushed actions (10 minutes) to cast the spell gives a +2 bonus.
 * Base difficulty for casting a spell is 4.
 * Using a focus (spell component, holy symbol, ritual, etc) gives a +1 bonus.
 * Failing a spell incurs a fatigue penalty equal to the MOF.
* Combat spells may be dodged: dodge MOS lowering the caster's MOS. (unless noted)

See the ''Spells'' section below for specific spell effects.

##  Shapeshifting 
Shapeshifting is enforcing the belief that one's body is a mutable edifice, not a static shell.
The caster has individual Form skills for each general shape. e.g.: Ursine, Feline, Canine, Shark, Raptor. The Complexity for the form is set by the GM. On a successful attempt, the caster takes on the shape, attributes, and natural abilities of the new form.
 * **Shapeshifter** ''(5 point perk)'' Allows the character to take Form skills. 
* Assuming a new form takes 3 actions.
 * The actions but must be consecutive.
 * The actions can be rushed as with normal Silhouette multiple-action rules.
 * The mutliple-action penalty for rushing a form change is only applied once.
 * Taking 6 non-rushed actions (1 minute) to assume the form gives a +1 bonus.
 * Taking 60 non-rushed actions (10 minutes) to assume the form gives a +2 bonus.
 * Base difficulty for assuming a form is 4.
* All the caster's skills in the new form are limited to a number of dice equal to the MOS of the shape roll
* The duration is concentration.
* Failing to assume a form incurs a fatigue penalty equal to the MOF.

The Shapeshifter may also take skills for Humanoid form and Regeneration.
 * The Humanoid skill allows them to change their appearance, and possibly even to mimic others.
 * Changing one's face is a CPX 1 task.
 * Changes to one's skin color or slight changes to body type (10% height increase or decrease, 20% mass increase or decrease) is a CPX 2 task.
 * Changes to one's race or significant changes to body type (25% height increase or decrease, 50% mass increase or decrease) is a CPX 3 task.
 * Vast changes to body type (50% height increase or decrease, up to 300% mass increase or 90% mass decrease) is a CPX 4 task.
 * If the skill is being used to mimic another person, the Shapeshifter can roll no more Humanoid skill dice than their Disguise skill rating.
 * The Regeneration skill allows the Shapeshifter to mend physical wounds and eventually to rid their bodies of toxins and even disease. 
 * As a CPX 1 task, the Shapeshifter can convert wounds into Fatigue, or lesser wounds. The amount of MOS needed to heal a wound is based on its penalty. A Flesh Wound requires an MOS of 1, and is converted to a -1 Fatigue penalty. A Deep Wound requires an MOS of 2, and is converted into a Flesh Wound and a -1 Fatigue penalty. 
 * To remove a drug or toxin from the Shapeshifter's system is a CPX 3 task. Subtract the MOS from the Potency of the toxin to determine the number of rounds it takes to purge the toxin from the Shapeshifter's system. 
 * To remove a disease from the Shapeshifter's system is a CPX 4 task. Subtract the MOS from the Virulence of the disease to determine the number of rounds it takes to purge the disease from the Shapeshifter's system. During the purging process, the Contagiousness of the disease is increased by the Shapeshifter's MOS.

##  Anscestor Magic 
Othalian magic comes from the power of one's ancestors. The greatest Othalians are heroes, like greek heroes. They are known for their deeds, and tales of their might and prowess might reach the far ends of the land in their lifetime. But to Othalians, lineage is as important as one's deeds.

When a hero dies, his legend grows unchecked by the concerns and weaknesses raised by mortality. So great is the regard the Othalians have for their heroes that their descendants can tap that legend to work wonders.

The descendant of Egil the Strong, who once threw a bolder the size of 5 men and defeated a troll in a test of strength, might call upon his ancestor's legendary might. The great grandson of Njal the Foresighted might summon up his forefather's wisdom, and plot a scheme to fool the wisest of kings.

I figure this would work like Tribe 8 magic, where the character's legendary ancestor possesses a particular trait, and the Hero (as Othalian magicians might be called) can attempt to perform incredible feats in keeping with that aspect (looser than a spell system, but also less widely applicable). The Perk might be 'Legendary Ancestor' which gives them access to 'Heroism' or 'Channelling' or something. They can buy the 5(?) point perk multiple times to get access to more heroes in their family tree. Heroes often become lords by their own hand, and much of the nobility of Othalia is of heroic blood.
